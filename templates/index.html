For Healthcare

Securely and fairly unify knowledge across institutions

Healthcare institutions using the same Electronic Medical Record (EMR) system share identical database schemas - creating a perfect opportunity for unified learning without data sharing. Reformer exploits this natural alignment to create a secure mesh across organizations, in which organizations' databases are virtually concatenated, enabling each institution to benefit from insights learned across billions of patient records while computation remains proportional to local data size, democratizing access to advanced healthcare intelligence.


Identify errors and inconsistencies before they cause harm

In an overwhelmed healthcare system, medical errors remain a leading cause of death, while coding errors cost billions. Reformer acts as a real-time error checker, leveraging patterns learned across billions of patient records to instantly flag potential anomalies or mistakes in diagnoses, prescriptions, and coding - catching dangerous errors before they impact patients.

For Defense

Securely eliminate silos

Intelligence silos cost lives. When critical information remains trapped within agencies, we miss vital connections that could prevent threats. Reformer eliminates this deadly compromise by creating a unified intelligence layer across databases with common identifiers, enabling organizations to detect threat patterns across systems. At the same time, data remains strictly controlled within security boundaries.


Acquire new perspective from partner data without observing it

Through Reformer's secure mesh, intelligence organizations with complementary data can discover new relationships in their records based on patterns learned from partner data - without ever seeing the data. For example, when one agency holds entity records and another tracks transactions between entities, both gain powerful new perspectives while their data remains secure within their systems, multiplying intelligence value while maintaining strict operational security and data sovereignty.

Frequently Asked Questions

Question: Do we have to join a mesh? Can we use Reformer only on our organizational data?

Answer: You do not have to join or create a mesh. Reformer's distributed model works just as well within your org, giving you access to expressive and robust Bayesian inference over databases with trillions of records

Question: Is Reformer another pre-trained LLM or foundation model trained on the world of data?

Answer: No. Reformer is a proprietary Bayesian Machine Learning model trained only on your data and the data in the mesh you are a member of.

Question: How long does training take and how much does it cost?

Answer: It varies. For example, training a model on a dataset with 1 billion records and 5 columns takes about 3 hours on a M4 Mackbook Pro, which would cost less than $4 on the equivalent on-demand AWS machine. Training time decreases with the number of machines. Only incremental updates are required to add new data and comply with the mesh once the initial model is trained.

Question: What kind of compute hardware does Reformer require?

Answer: Reformer works on your (or your cloud provider's) 64-bit x86 and ARM harware. No special computing hardware like GPUs or TPUs are required. If your data does not fit in the memory of one machine, it will be distributed to multiple, potentially heterogeneous machines.
